\title{Projective Transcorrelated Mixed-Reference Spin-Flip
Configuration Interaction Singles (pTC--MRSF-CIS):\\
Non-dynamical and dynamical correlation for excited states at
$\mathcal{O}(N^5)$}

\author{C.~H.~Choi}
\email[Corresponding author: ]{cheolho.choi@gmail.com}
\affiliation{Department of Chemistry, Kyungpook National University}
\author{S.~Ten-no}
\email[Corresponding author: ]{tenno@garnet.kobe-u.ac.jp}
\affiliation{Graduate School of System Informatics, Kobe University, Kobe 657-8501, Japan}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Mixed-reference spin-flip (MRSF) methods deliver spin-pure, multireference-quality
descriptions of ground and excited states---diradicals, bond dissociation,
doubly-excited states, and the correct topology of conical intersections,
including the $S_0/S_1$ seam---within a single-reference, configuration-interaction-singles
(CIS) cost. At the bare Hartree--Fock level, however, MRSF-CIS lacks dynamic
correlation and does not describe the electron--electron cusp, so excitation
energies are systematically biased and basis-set convergence is slow. We propose
\emph{projective transcorrelated} MRSF-CIS (pTC--MRSF-CIS): the bare electronic
Hamiltonian is replaced by Ten-no's non-Hermitian projective transcorrelated
effective Hamiltonian $\Hbar=\ee^{-\tau}H\,\ee^{\tau}$, which injects dynamic
correlation and the spin-resolved cusp directly into the Hamiltonian, without a
density functional and without the double-counting problem that hampers
transcorrelated Kohn--Sham theory. Because the projective transcorrelation
satisfies the singlet \emph{and} triplet first-order cusp conditions
simultaneously and is free of spin contamination, it is uniquely matched to the
spin purity of the mixed reference. We show that the resulting method retains the
$\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ (MP2-class) scaling of MRSF-CIS provided the three- and
four-body terms of $\Hbar$ are normal-ordered against the reference density and
density-fitted. The transcorrelated response matrix is non-Hermitian; we present
a general (non-symmetric) reduced-space eigensolver returning real eigenvalues
with biorthonormal left/right Ritz vectors, validated to reproduce the symmetric
limit to machine precision. Proof-of-concept calculations on methylene
demonstrate that the spin-flip construction reproduces complete-active-space
configuration interaction in the active space and yields spin-pure states, and an
exactly-solvable Hubbard dimer shows that a single mean-field determinant
evaluated with $\Hbar$ recovers the full correlation energy. We argue that
pTC--MRSF-CIS occupies a distinctive niche relative to ADC(2): equal
$\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ scaling and comparable dynamic correlation, but with the
non-dynamical correlation and correct conical-intersection topology that
single-reference perturbation theories lack.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The accurate and affordable description of electronically excited states remains
a central challenge of quantum chemistry, particularly in regions of strong
non-dynamical correlation: diradicals, bond breaking, doubly-excited states, and
conical intersections that govern nonadiabatic dynamics. Linear-response
time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) is inexpensive but fails for
doubly-excited states and yields an incorrect dimensionality of the $S_0/S_1$
intersection seam. Single-reference wavefunction response methods such as
ADC(2)~\cite{schirmer1982,dreuw2015} and CC2 capture dynamic correlation at
$\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ but inherit the breakdown of the underlying M{\o}ller--Plesset
ground state in multireference regions. Multireference methods (CASPT2, MRCI,
NEVPT2) are accurate but costly and require active-space selection.

The spin-flip (SF) family~\cite{krylov2001} offers a single-reference route to
multireference targets: low-spin states are reached by spin-flipping excitations
from a high-spin reference. Standard SF-CIS/SF-TDDFT, however, suffers from spin
contamination because the spin-flip manifold generated from a single reference is
spin-incomplete. The mixed-reference spin-flip
(MRSF)~\cite{lee2018,horbatenko2021} construction restores spin completeness by
combining the $M_s=\pm1$ components of the triplet reference, yielding spin-pure
singlet and triplet states, an $S_0$ described on the same footing as the excited
states, and the correct topology of conical intersections---all at CIS cost.

What MRSF-CIS lacks, at the bare Hartree--Fock level, is dynamic correlation and a
description of the interelectronic cusp. In practice this is supplied by a
density functional (MRSF-TDDFT), at the price of functional dependence and a
double-counting ambiguity if one later wishes to add explicit correlation. Here
we pursue a different route. The transcorrelated (TC)
method~\cite{boys1969,tenno2000} similarity-transforms the Hamiltonian with a
correlation factor, building explicit electron correlation into the operator
rather than the wavefunction. Ten-no's recent \emph{projective}
transcorrelation~\cite{tenno2023} (pTC) is particularly attractive: its effective
Hamiltonian terminates formally at four-body interactions, is free of spin
contamination, and satisfies the singlet and triplet first-order cusp conditions
simultaneously. These are exactly the properties required to complement the
spin-pure mixed reference.

We propose pTC--MRSF-CIS, in which the MRSF-CIS response is built from the
non-Hermitian projective transcorrelated Hamiltonian. The method adds dynamic
correlation and cusp behaviour to MRSF-CIS, retains $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ scaling,
avoids any functional and the attendant double counting, and is constructed so
that the spin-resolved cusp of pTC is matched to the spin purity of MRSF.

\section{Theory}

\subsection{MRSF-CIS}

Let $\op{a}^\dagger_{p\sigma}$, $\op{a}_{p\sigma}$ denote creation/annihilation
operators for spatial orbital $p$ and spin $\sigma$. MRSF builds target $M_s=0$
states by spin-flipping excitations acting on the two components
$\ket{T_{+}}$ ($M_s=+1$) and $\ket{T_{-}}$ ($M_s=-1$) of a high-spin
restricted-open-shell (ROHF) triplet reference whose two singly-occupied frontier
orbitals are denoted $O_1,O_2$. The response states are
\begin{equation}
\ket{\Psi_I}=\sum_{ia}\, c^{I}_{ia}\,
\op{a}^\dagger_{a\beta}\op{a}_{i\alpha}\,\ket{T_{+}}
+ (\,+\!\leftrightarrow\!-\,),
\end{equation}
where the mixed-reference combination restores spin completeness. The working
equation is a Tamm--Dancoff (CIS-like) Hermitian eigenproblem
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{A}\,\mathbf{c}^I=\omega_I\,\mathbf{c}^I,
\qquad \mathbf{A}=\mathbf{A}^{\mathsf{T}},
\label{eq:mrsf}
\end{equation}
solved by a symmetric Davidson iteration. The matrix--vector product
$\mathbf{A}\mathbf{c}$ is a Fock-like contraction of two-electron integrals,
scaling $\mathcal{O}(N^4)$ per iteration.

\subsection{Projective transcorrelation}

The transcorrelated Hamiltonian is the non-unitary similarity transform
\begin{equation}
\Hbar=\ee^{-\tau}\,H\,\ee^{\tau},
\label{eq:tc}
\end{equation}
with $\tau$ a (symmetric, real) correlator encoding the interelectronic cusp.
Using the Baker--Campbell--Hausdorff expansion,
\begin{equation}
\Hbar=H+[H,\tau]+\tfrac12[[H,\tau],\tau]+\dots,
\end{equation}
which, for a two-body $\tau$, terminates exactly because $H$ contains at most
two-body operators. In Ten-no's projective transcorrelation~\cite{tenno2023} the
correlator is fixed by the first-order cusp conditions for both singlet
(antiparallel-spin, coalescence cusp $\tfrac12$) and triplet (parallel-spin,
$\tfrac14$) coalescence, simultaneously, and the resulting $\Hbar$ is free of
spin contamination and terminates formally at four-body interactions. The price
is that $\Hbar$ is \emph{non-Hermitian}.

\subsection{Transcorrelated MRSF-CIS}

Replacing $H$ by $\Hbar$ in the MRSF-CIS construction yields the transcorrelated
response matrix $\bar{\mathbf{A}}$, which is no longer symmetric. The eigenproblem
becomes
\begin{equation}
\bar{\mathbf{A}}\,\mathbf{c}^I_{\!R}=\omega_I\,\mathbf{c}^I_{\!R},
\qquad
\bar{\mathbf{A}}^{\mathsf{T}}\,\mathbf{c}^I_{\!L}=\omega_I\,\mathbf{c}^I_{\!L},
\label{eq:tcmrsf}
\end{equation}
with distinct right and left eigenvectors obeying the biorthonormality condition
$\mathbf{c}^{I}_{\!L}\!\cdot\mathbf{c}^{J}_{\!R}=\delta_{IJ}$. For bound states
the eigenvalues $\omega_I$ are real; complex eigenvalues signal a non-Hermitian
instability and are monitored.

Everything that makes MRSF-CIS attractive---the mixed-reference spin completeness,
$S_0$ as a response root, and the correct conical-intersection topology---is
untouched, because the spin-flip construction and the reference are unchanged.
What changes is the operator inside the matrix build and the symmetry of the
eigenproblem.

\subsection{Spin-resolved cusp and the choice of reference}

The cusp condition is spin-dependent. A spin-contaminated SF-CIS state, being a
mixture of singlet and triplet, satisfies neither the singlet nor the triplet
cusp cleanly, so a transcorrelation built for a definite spin state is applied to
an indefinite-spin object. The mixed-reference spin purity of MRSF removes this
mismatch: each state has a well-defined spin and therefore a well-defined cusp
that coincides with what pTC corrects. This is the central reason MRSF, rather
than single-reference SF-CIS, is the appropriate substrate for projective
transcorrelation.

\subsection{Scaling and the higher-body terms}

The non-Hermitian eigenproblem~\eqref{eq:tcmrsf} is solved by a general
(non-symmetric) Davidson iteration of the same $\mathcal{O}(N^4)$/iteration cost
as the symmetric one; carrying left and right vectors increases the prefactor but
not the order. The rate-limiting step is the construction of $\Hbar$. Its genuine
three- and four-body terms are never materialized: they are normal-ordered
against the ROHF reference one-particle density, folding their dominant
contributions into effective one- and two-body integrals, and the correlation
factor is density-fitted (resolution of the identity). With this treatment the
$\Hbar$ build is $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$, consistent with the cost of pTC(2) being
approximately half that of MP2-F12~\cite{tenno2023}. Explicit three-body
integrals would be $\mathcal{O}(N^6)$ and explicit four-body $\mathcal{O}(N^8)$;
the normal-ordering/density-fitting discipline is therefore essential and is the
single design decision that keeps the method at MP2 scaling. Sub-$N^5$ scaling is
in principle accessible because the correlation factor is short-ranged, via
local/pair-natural-orbital or tensor-hypercontraction techniques, but these are
deferred optimizations.

The final scaling is summarized as follows: ROHF reference, $\mathcal{O}(N^4)$;
integral transformation, $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$; $\Hbar$ build (normal-ordered,
density-fitted), $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ (rate limiting); non-Hermitian Davidson,
$\mathcal{O}(N^4)$/iteration. Thus pTC--MRSF-CIS is an $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$,
MP2-class method.

\section{Non-Hermitian reduced eigensolver}

The reduced-space (Davidson subspace) diagonalization is the one genuinely new
numerical kernel. Given a non-symmetric reduced matrix $\mathbf{A}_r$ we compute
the right and left eigenvectors in a single DGEEV pass, sort the (real) bound-state
eigenvalues, and rescale so that $\mathbf{c}_L^I\!\cdot\mathbf{c}_R^I=1$. With the
transcorrelation switched off ($\tau=0$), $\mathbf{A}_r$ is symmetric and the
kernel reproduces the symmetric solver to machine precision---the acceptance gate
for all of the non-Hermitian infrastructure. In our reference implementation this
gate is met at $\max|\Delta\omega|\sim7\times10^{-15}$~Ha, and a non-symmetric
test matrix is solved with right/left residuals and biorthonormality error of
order $10^{-14}$.

We illustrate the building blocks with two exactly-controllable systems.

\paragraph{Methylene (real integrals).}
For \ce{CH2} in a $6$-$31$G basis with a $(2,2)$ active space, the MRSF-CIS
spin-flip determinant Hamiltonian built from genuine molecular integrals
reproduces the independent complete-active-space configuration interaction,
CASCI$(2,2)$, ground-state energy to $<10^{-8}$~Ha, and yields spin-pure states
($\langle S^2\rangle=0$ for singlets, $2$ for the triplet). The transcorrelated
non-Hermitian solve on the same real integrals reproduces the MRSF-CIS spectrum at
$\tau=0$ and, for a non-symmetric $\Hbar$, returns a real spectrum with
biorthonormal left/right states (residuals $\sim10^{-14}$), demonstrating that the
machinery operates on real molecular integrals.

\paragraph{Hubbard dimer (correlation recovery).}
A complete active space cannot reveal correlation \emph{recovery}, because a
similarity transform preserves the spectrum. We therefore use the half-filled
two-site Hubbard model, whose exact ground-state energy is
$E_0=\tfrac12\!\left(U-\sqrt{U^2+16t^2}\right)$. A single mean-field (RHF)
determinant has $E_{\mathrm{RHF}}=\tfrac{U}{2}-2t>E_0$. Evaluating the
\emph{transcorrelated} Hamiltonian $\Hbar=J^{-1}HJ$ in that same mean-field
reference, with a Gutzwiller correlation factor $J=g^{\op{D}}$
($\op{D}$ the double-occupancy operator), recovers $100\%$ of the correlation
energy at the optimal $g$; equivalently, the mean-field determinant becomes the
exact right-eigenvector of $\Hbar$, $\Hbar\ket{g^2}=E_0\ket{g^2}$. This is the
mechanism pTC--MRSF-CIS exploits: a compact (CIS-like) reference evaluated with
the transcorrelated Hamiltonian captures correlation absent from the same compact
reference under the bare Hamiltonian.

\section{Relation to ADC(2)}

ADC(2) shares the $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ scaling of pTC--MRSF-CIS and provides
second-order dynamic correlation, but it is single-reference: it inherits the
M{\o}ller--Plesset ground state and therefore lacks non-dynamical correlation,
omits doubly-excited states at strict second order, and produces an incorrect
$S_0/S_1$ intersection topology. pTC--MRSF-CIS supplies the non-dynamical
correlation and correct topology through the mixed reference while matching the
dynamic correlation through transcorrelation, at equal scaling. The intended
advantage is therefore specific: not a uniform improvement over ADC(2)---which
remains mature, Hermitian, rigorously size-extensive, and excellent for
single-reference valence and Rydberg states---but superior performance in the
multireference regime (diradicals, bond breaking, doubly-excited states, conical
intersections) where ADC(2) is known to fail, at the same $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ cost.

\section{Conclusions}

pTC--MRSF-CIS combines the spin-pure, multireference-capable, topology-correct
description of MRSF-CIS with the dynamic correlation and cusp behaviour of Ten-no's
projective transcorrelation, without a density functional and without double
counting, at $\mathcal{O}(N^5)$ scaling. The spin-resolved cusp conditions of pTC
are matched to the spin purity of the mixed reference, which is what singles out
MRSF (rather than SF-CIS) as the substrate. The principal new numerical ingredient,
a non-Hermitian reduced-space eigensolver, has been validated against its
symmetric limit, and the spin-flip construction and correlation-recovery mechanism
have been demonstrated on real molecular integrals and an exactly-solvable model.
Outstanding work comprises the density-fitted transcorrelated integral build, the
normal-ordering of the three- and four-body terms, and integration into the
analytic-gradient infrastructure. A state-averaged CAS$(4,4)$ reference, which
removes the residual ROHF reference ambiguity and presents pTC with a genuine
small full-CI solver, is a natural longer-term substrate.
